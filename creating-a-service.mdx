title: "Creating a service"
---

<Warning>
  Please ensure you have read and agree to the requirements and also our privacy policy.
</Warning>

To create a service on James Auth, ensure you have access to the Supabase database. You automatically gain access to this once you have gained consent for your app to be on James Auth.

<Card title="Database access" type="note" horizontal>
  You may not be able to access the database. If that is the case, James will edit the data for you.
</Card>

1. Head over to the database on Supabase.
2. Click on "Tables"
3. Next to services, click the "View in Table editor" button.
4. Under insert, select 'insert row'

5. On the modal that opens, leave id blank, and fill in your full app's name under 'name'
6. Under short\_name, fill out your application's short name. More info can be found in [Requirements](/requirements).
7. Next, add an app description (recommended). This should explain what your app does and how it uses with James Auth.
8. Under icon\_class add an fa icon. All icons follow this format:
   ```text
   fa-[icon name]
   ```
9. Leave accent colour blank.
10. Under launch\_url add your website. Although not required, it can help direct users to your site.
    <img
      src="/images/req2.png"
      alt="Req2"
      title="Req2"
      style={{ width:"45%" }}
    />
    11. If your service is in beta, under is\_beta, change this to TRUE. You can change this at any time.
    12. If you want to redirect users to a custom page after successful authentication, change this to your custom page e.g. after successful auth you could link to [https://yoursite.com/authsuccess.html](https://yoursite.com/authsuccess.html), and recieve the data there (beta). Or, if you want to recieve the data via PostMessage, so the window closes and you continue the auth process on the same page it started, leave it blank.
    13. Under privacy\_url, if your site is using the storedatacloud scope (syncing user data other than user info to your database), you must include a privacy policy. For more info, see [scopes](/scopes).
    14. If you wish to show a custom note on the James Auth dashboard, fill in the note\_from\_developer field.
    15. Click Save changes, and now you're ready to make a request!
